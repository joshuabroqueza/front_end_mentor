What your BMI result means

A healthy BMI range is considered to be between 18.5 to 24.9. This range indicates that the weight of an individual is within a healthy range for their height. It is important to maintain a healthy weight as it can contribute to a better quality of life and reduce the risk of various health conditions such as heart disease, stroke and diabetes. To maintain a healthy weight, it is recommended to follow a balanced diet and engage in regular physical activity. Moreover, a healthy weight is not just about looking good, but it is also necessary for optimal physical and mental well-being.

Healthy Eating

Healthy eating promotes weight control, disease prevention, better digestion, immunity, mental clarity, and modd.

Regular Exercise

Exercise improves fitness, aids weight control, elevates mood, and reduces disease risk, fostering wellness and longevity.

Adequate Sleep

Sleep enhances mental clarity, emotional stability, and physical wellness, promoting overall restoration and rejuvenation.

Limitations of BMI

Although BMI is often a practical indicator of healthy weight, it is not suited for every person. Specific groups should carefully consider their BMI outcomes, and in certain cases, the measurement may not beneficial to use.

Gender

The development and body fat composition of girls and boys vary with age. Consequently, a child's age and gender are considered when evaluating their BMI.

Age

In aging individuals, increased body fat and muscle loss may cause BMI to underestimate body fat content.

Muscle

BMI may misclassify muscular individuals as overweight or obese, as it doesn't differentiate muscle from fat.

Pregnancy

Expectant mothers experience weight gain due to their growing baby. Maintaining a healthy pre-pregnancy BMI is advisable to minimise health risks for both mother and child.

Race

Certain health concerns may effect individuals of some Black and Asian origins at lower BMIs than others. To learn more, it is advised to discuss this with your GP or practice nurse.
